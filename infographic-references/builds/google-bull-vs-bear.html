BullWhy Google is winning
  • Gemini 3 is #1. Launched as the top-ranked model in the world — leads every track on LMArena.
  • ChatGPT is losing ground. Its share slid from ~76% to ~54%, bleeding to both Gemini (~28%) and Claude — the fastest-growing assistant.
  • Perplexity fizzled. Downloads down ~80% from their peak; the "Chrome killer" browser threat faded.
  • TPUs go external. Meta in talks to buy billions of Google's chips by 2027 — Nvidia fired back a defensive statement.
  • A cash machine. >$100B/yr in profit lets Google run Gemini at a loss to bleed OpenAI dry.
  • OpenAI's math is scary. Projected to lose $76.5B by 2030 and needs to raise >$200B to get there.
  • Berkshire bought in. A new $4.3B stake — its first-ever position in Google.
  • Pulling ahead of the pack. Now outperforming the "OpenAI gang" — Oracle, Microsoft and Nvidia.
Bull bottom lineThe AI-winner story is real — and getting stronger.
BearThe valuation catch
  • Most expensive in 16 years. 25.2× operating cash flow vs a 17.5× long-term average.
  • Re-rating, not earnings. Since March: cash flow grew +14.2%, but the multiple expanded +77.6%.
  • The easy money's done. To double again the multiple must hit ~50× — "not going to happen."
  • Base case ≈ 9%/yr. And that needs cash flow to nearly double ($151B → ~$300B) over five years.
  • Even the bull DCF ≈ 14%/yr. At 17% growth and a 20× multiple — barely beats the S&P 500.
  • Buy fear, not euphoria. It was 14× and hated in early 2025. Today it's loved and priced for it.
  • The move looks done. The video's author isn't buying up here — he sees better risk/reward elsewhere.
Bear bottom lineYou're paying a premium for what's now the consensus view.
Myth

"Buffett is buying the top!" Not so. Berkshire bought during Q3 at ≤$243 (maybe as low as ~$175), not the peak — and it was likely a younger portfolio manager, not Buffett himself (Google sits outside his circle of competence).
